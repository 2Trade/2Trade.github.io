Firebase Mixtape

Getting firebase online took way too many tries.

Posted on July 7, 2016

Firebase Chatroom

Today we used Firebase to try and make a chatroom we could put on our website. We learned that making a simple chatroom would take a lot more time and effort than what we previously expected. Firebase relies heavily on terminal/sublime text, and also requires the downloading of many different applications to work. Everything from the creation of a user sign-in system to the "send message" system, every part took much more effort than we expected when we were told to make this.

Firebase tutorial: How to make a Friendly Chatroom using Firebase

Link to tutorial: https://codelabs.developers.google.com/codelabs/firebase-web/#0

1. Set up the chat.
Before doing anything, make sure all of the sample code is in place, and install Firebase CLI(Command Line Interface).
2. Add Firebase to the app.
Go to the Firebase console, click "Create New Project", then click "Add Firebase to your Web App" copy down the code, then put it into your app's console.
3. Test-Run the App
Now that Firebase is on the app, test it out. Run this code:
$web-start> firebase serve
Listening at http://localhost:5000
4. Designing Sign-In.
No one can use your app if it doesn't have any sort of sign-in feature! Type this code:
FriendlyChat.prototype.initFirebase = function() {
this.auth = firebase.auth();
this.database = firebase.database();
this.storage = firebase.storage();
this.auth.onAuthStateChanged(this.onAuthStateChanged.bind(this));
}
5. Test your top sketches.
Pick the top 3 concepts and share them with family and friends. Be prepared for honest feedback. Once the concept is finalized, turn the drawing into a usable digital format.
6. Develop logo layout.
Make sure that all of your text and shapes are perfectly spaced out and lined up.Your logo doesn’t have to be symmetrical, but the spacing does need to make sense.
7. Pick versatile color options.
Eventually your logo will be used on a black or colored background. Always be sure to have logo color options for a dark background as well as for a light one. That might mean only have to changing the color of your font or in some cases you may have to change the color of your entire logo.
8. Choose a font.
Stay away from fonts that are generic and on every computer out there. Some examples of generic fonts are Times New Roman and Comic Sans. Those fonts will only work against you and your company by making you less memorable.
9. Ensure scalability.
Your logo needs to be able to be scaled into any size, large enough for billboards and small enough for business cards. It’s important that regardless of the size, that every part of your logo is legible.
